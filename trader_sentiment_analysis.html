07 / Key Insights & Hidden Patterns
🔍 Fear Outperforms Greed in Volume PnL
Fear days generated $3.36M total PnL — more than any other sentiment. While Extreme Greed has the highest per-trade average ($130), the sheer volume of trades in Fear (29,808) makes it the highest revenue regime. The real alpha is in fearful markets, not euphoric ones.
📉 Greed Days Are Riskier Than Fear Days
Greed has a lower win rate (76.9%) than Fear (87.3%) and produces more big losses (132 vs 85 losses over $1K). Overconfidence in bull sentiment leads to poor trade management, inflating position sizes at the wrong time.
📊 Near-Perfect Win Rate is Achievable
Trader 0xbaaa… achieved a 99% win rate across 9,997 trades. This suggests systematic strategies — likely tight stop management, scalping small targets, and avoiding overnight holds in volatile sentiment regimes.
🔄 49% Contrarian Behavior Detected
Nearly half of opening positions go against prevailing sentiment. Top traders systematically short Greed and buy Fear, suggesting the cohort as a whole is more sophisticated than typical retail sentiment-followers.
📌 Long Bias Peaks in Extreme Fear (3:1)
When the market is most fearful, traders go aggressively long at a 3:1 long-to-short ratio. This compresses to near-parity (1.14:1) in Extreme Greed — the classic "buy the dip" behavior is measurably present here.
🌙 December & February Are Peak Months
Post-halving (Q2) and post-election (Q4) macro cycles dominate performance. August was the only losing month, coinciding with the late summer crypto lull and macro fears around US recession signals.
⚡ Extreme Fear Drives Highest Daily Activity
743 trades/day on average during Extreme Fear vs only 150 during Greed. Volatility creates opportunity — experienced traders ramp up frequency precisely when markets are most chaotic.
🎯 Short Exits Struggle in Greed (68.9% WR)
Close Short trades during Greed regime only win 68.9% of the time — the worst close-type/sentiment combo. Avoid shorting into momentum. The market doesn't reverse just because sentiment is elevated.
